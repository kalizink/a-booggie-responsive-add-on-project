Why A Boogie Wit da Hoodie is the best

His most recent album, Hoodie SZN, spent two weeks atop the Billboard 200—a milestone for the homegrown rapper from the Bronx’s Highbridge neighborhood.

“I know it’s like I’m supposed to be all excited, but that’s not me,” he says in between bites of a cheese puff. With hundreds of songs recorded and at the ready and a growing number of soundalikes on his heels, he feels that this is no time to celebrate for too long: “I give myself a pat on the back, then keep on pushing.”

Though he would never give himself the title, an argument could be made that A Boogie is the current holder of rap’s contested “King of New York” title—at least for his generation of artists.
